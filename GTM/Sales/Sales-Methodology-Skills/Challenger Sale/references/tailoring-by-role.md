# Tailoring by Role — Economic Drivers Reference

The Challenger attribute *Tailor* exists because the same insight has to
land differently depending on who's in the room. A reframe that moves a
CFO has to be re-threaded to move a VP Eng, a CISO, or a COO — same
underlying insight, different economic lens.

Use this reference to produce a **per-stakeholder economic-impact snippet**
— what each role cares about, how their P&L or KPI actually moves, and
what the "so what" looks like in their language.

**Critical framing** (from the deck): tailoring is **an organizational
capability**, not a rep skill. Marketing / sales ops should produce these
maps centrally. This doc is Alex's personal version to use live.

---

## Tailoring layers (broad → narrow)

For every stakeholder, tailor at four nested layers. Start broad, narrow
down. Never skip:

1. **Industry** — macro pressures, regulations, competitive dynamics.
2. **Company** — this specific company's strategy, stage, public
   commitments.
3. **Role** — what this seat is on the hook for (the rows below).
4. **Individual** — this specific person's priorities, their personal
   wins, their political risks.

Most reps skip layers 2 and 4. That's where tailoring actually lives.

---

## CEO / Founder

- **Measured on**: company growth rate, market position, board confidence,
  ability to fundraise, runway, strategic optionality.
- **Time horizon**: 18–36 months.
- **Dominant fear**: irrelevance, category shift they missed, running out
  of money, losing to a competitor they should have beaten.
- **Buying authority**: can override the org when it matters; usually won't
  get involved in an evaluation unless it's strategic.
- **Reframe language**: "Category-defining bet," "moat," "window is
  closing," "this is the thing your next board meeting should be about."
- **Don't say**: cost savings, productivity gains, line-item ROI.
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "If [reframe insight] is right, the companies that act in the next
  > 12 months own this category for the next 5. Here's what that looks
  > like for [company's public commitment]."

---

## CFO / VP Finance

- **Measured on**: gross margin, cash burn, predictability of forecasts,
  cost per dollar of revenue, audit cleanliness.
- **Time horizon**: this quarter and next.
- **Dominant fear**: a surprise in the quarterly close, an unbudgeted line
  item, a forecast miss, a vendor contract that turns out to be more than
  they thought.
- **Buying authority**: controls discretionary spend; often the actual
  Economic Buyer.
- **Reframe language**: "Unbudgeted exposure," "hidden liability,"
  "predictable run-rate cost vs. variable surprise," "cost of inaction."
- **Don't say**: "it's cheaper than the competitor" (they assume you'd say
  that); "it pays for itself" (with no math).
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "The real exposure isn't the price of [solution] — it's the [quantified
  > hidden cost] you're absorbing today without a line item for it. Here's
  > what that looks like annualized: [number]."
- **Special rule**: the ROI math must be validated by *their* finance team,
  not yours. Hand the model to the CFO's FP&A lead to stress-test. A model
  the buyer doesn't own dies in committee.

---

## CRO / VP Sales

- **Measured on**: attainment, pipeline coverage, ramp time, rep retention,
  forecast accuracy.
- **Time horizon**: current quarter, next quarter.
- **Dominant fear**: missing the number; coverage ratio deteriorating;
  forecast dropping mid-quarter; top reps leaving.
- **Reframe language**: "Coverage," "ramp," "rep productivity," "forecast
  risk," "deal velocity."
- **Don't say**: generic productivity; they'll assume you're selling time
  savings, which doesn't move their number.
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "Every week ramp takes longer, you lose [N] pipeline days per rep per
  > quarter. Across [team size], that's [X] in attainment at current
  > close rates. Here's what that compounds to over a full fiscal year."

---

## CIO / CTO / VP Engineering

- **Measured on**: uptime, system velocity, engineering productivity,
  platform scalability, technical-debt ratio, talent retention.
- **Time horizon**: current quarter for incidents, 12–24 months for
  platform bets.
- **Dominant fear**: outage during an earnings call; a platform bet that
  doesn't scale; losing top engineers to a competitor; being the one who
  approved a tool that failed.
- **Reframe language**: "Engineer hours reclaimed," "time-to-merge,"
  "build-vs-buy math," "platform resilience," "scalability at 10x."
- **Don't say**: "faster" without units; "easier to use" (not a buyer
  driver for this role).
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "Your fully-loaded engineer costs ~[$X/yr]. If [reframe insight] saves
  > [N hours/wk] per eng, across [team size] that's [$Y/yr]. More
  > importantly, it unlocks [specific roadmap item that's currently
  > stalled]."

---

## CISO / VP Security

- **Measured on**: incident count and severity, audit findings, regulatory
  posture, board-reportable risk, mean time to detect / respond.
- **Time horizon**: audit cycles + always-on incident risk.
- **Dominant fear**: the breach that ends up in the press; the audit
  finding they can't explain; the regulatory fine; being the CISO when a
  SOX or SOC 2 fails.
- **Reframe language**: "Audit exposure," "board-reportable risk,"
  "blast radius," "mean time to detect," "policy violation count."
- **Don't say**: "makes security easier"; "nobody else has been breached
  by this" (they assume it's inevitable).
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "The question isn't whether [reframed threat] will land. It's whether
  > when it does, you can show the board you had the controls in place.
  > Here's what 'yes' looks like. Here's what 'no' cost [peer company]
  > last year."

---

## COO / VP Operations

- **Measured on**: throughput, unit economics, SLA adherence, quality,
  headcount efficiency, process reliability.
- **Time horizon**: monthly ops reviews; quarterly capacity plans.
- **Dominant fear**: throughput collapse during a scale event; quality
  dropping; customer-facing SLAs slipping; surprise headcount request.
- **Reframe language**: "Throughput per FTE," "SLA adherence,"
  "cost-to-serve," "quality at scale," "process reliability."
- **Don't say**: generic "efficiency"; "it's better."
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "At your current [throughput unit], the bottleneck between now and
  > [scale target] is [specific process]. Every week it's unfixed,
  > you're adding [$X] to cost-to-serve or delaying [launch/SLA]. Here's
  > the compounding math."

---

## CMO / VP Marketing

- **Measured on**: pipeline sourced, marketing-qualified leads, CAC,
  brand metrics, campaign ROI, content velocity.
- **Time horizon**: campaign-cycle (4–8 weeks) + quarterly pipeline
  commits.
- **Dominant fear**: a pipeline miss that bleeds into the sales number;
  CAC climbing quarter over quarter; a campaign that flops publicly.
- **Reframe language**: "Pipeline coverage," "CAC payback," "content
  velocity," "campaign attribution," "brand lift."
- **Don't say**: "more leads" generically.
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "At current CAC, every [$1] of pipeline costs you [$X]. If [reframe
  > insight] shortens the cycle by [N%], that's [$Y] saved per [period]
  > — without needing more headcount."

---

## CHRO / VP People

- **Measured on**: retention, time-to-fill, employee engagement, comp
  competitiveness, DEI metrics.
- **Time horizon**: annual cycles with always-on attrition risk.
- **Dominant fear**: regretted attrition of top performers; a compliance
  failure in comp or hiring; an engagement drop before a funding round.
- **Reframe language**: "Regretted attrition," "time-to-productivity,"
  "engagement drivers," "comp competitiveness," "manager effectiveness."
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "Replacing a regretted [role] costs roughly [1.5–2× salary] fully
  > loaded. At your current attrition, that's [$X/yr]. [Reframe] reduces
  > that by [N%] — here's what that looks like across a full year."

---

## General Counsel / Legal

- **Measured on**: contract risk, compliance posture, litigation exposure,
  MSA turnaround, regulatory readiness.
- **Time horizon**: case-by-case on contracts; always-on on risk.
- **Dominant fear**: a clause that exposes the company in a way the CEO
  finds out about from outside counsel.
- **Reframe language**: "Contract risk concentration," "indemnity
  exposure," "regulatory readiness," "MSA throughput."
- **Special rule** (from MEDDPICC's Paper Process): Legal is where deals
  slip 30–60 days. Get them in the room at verbal yes, not at redline.

---

## Procurement

- **Measured on**: savings achieved, contract compliance, vendor
  consolidation, cycle time.
- **Time horizon**: fiscal year savings target.
- **Dominant fear**: approving a deal at list price that another company
  bought for less; being the one who didn't push back.
- **Reframe language**: "Total cost of ownership," "vendor consolidation,"
  "term structure," "year-over-year price protection."
- **Rule of engagement**: they are trained to extract. Don't negotiate
  price here — renegotiate scope. Anchor on value terms the EB already
  agreed to. Procurement cannot undo an EB's business decision; they can
  only shave the edges.

---

## VP Product / CPO

- **Measured on**: adoption, retention, NPS, feature velocity, roadmap
  delivery, monetization.
- **Time horizon**: release cycles + OKR quarters.
- **Dominant fear**: a roadmap miss that makes the leadership team lose
  confidence in product; a feature that doesn't adopt; a competitor
  shipping something they're still scoping.
- **Reframe language**: "Adoption curves," "time-to-value," "retention
  lift," "roadmap leverage," "build-vs-buy."
- **Economic-impact snippet template**:
  > "If [reframe] is right, [roadmap item] gets [N] months of leverage
  > sooner. That's [business metric the VP Product owns] moving [X]
  > quarters earlier than current plan."

## How to use this reference in a live pitch

For each named stakeholder, produce a 3-line snippet:

1. **One-line role driver**: what they're measured on in *this* company.
2. **One-line reframe translation**: how the universal insight maps into
   their accountability.
3. **One-line economic impact**: the quantified delta in their units
   (dollars, days, headcount, throughput, risk).

Paste all snippets into the deck as a single **Tailored Economic Impact**
appendix slide — one row per stakeholder. This slide is what your
Mobilizer circulates internally; it does the pre-work of building
consensus across the 5.4-person buying committee.
