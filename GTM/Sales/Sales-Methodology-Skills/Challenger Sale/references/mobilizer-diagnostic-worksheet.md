# Mobilizer Diagnostic Worksheet

Used to classify every named stakeholder on a deal into the 7 customer types
from *The Challenger Customer* (Adamson, Dixon, Spenner, Toman, 2015). The
point is brutal: **only Mobilizers can close deals with a buying committee
of 5.4 stakeholders (CEB avg).** Talkers feel like progress; they aren't.

## The 7 types at a glance

| Bucket | Type | One-line tell |
|---|---|---|
| **Mobilizer** | Go-Getter | Results-oriented; acts on good ideas immediately; speaks in specifics |
| **Mobilizer** | Teacher | Persuasive; loves sharing insight; influences peers |
| **Mobilizer** | Skeptic | Challenges everything; hard to build rapport with; durable advocate once convinced |
| **Talker** | Friend | Pleasant and responsive; no internal power |
| **Talker** | Guide | Shares information readily; won't advocate |
| **Talker** | Climber | Self-interested; backs whoever is winning |
| **Blocker** | Blocker | Actively obstructs; route around |

**Mobilizer math**: at 5.4 stakeholders per deal, consensus — not a single
champion — is what closes. Only Mobilizers build consensus. A Talker
champion = a deal that stalls at committee.

## The diagnostic question

Run this on every named contact early in the cycle:

> *"What are the top 2 things you'd need to see change in the next 12
> months?"*

- **Mobilizers** answer with **specific, time-bound, consequential** items.
  ("We need to cut onboarding time from 8 weeks to 3 by Q3 or we'll miss
  the Europe launch.")
- **Talkers** answer with **generics**. ("Efficiency. Innovation.
  Alignment.")

Run it. Write down the answer. Score below.

## Common errors this worksheet catches

- **Champion inflation** — calling a Friend a champion because they're
  responsive. Friends give you *access*, not *advancement*.
- **Skeptic dismissal** — writing off a Skeptic as "not a fit" because they
  push back. Skeptics become the most durable advocates once convinced —
  keep investing.
- **Climber misreading** — Climbers look like Mobilizers in the first
  meeting (they're charming and ambitious). The tell: they hedge once the
  deal gets visible internally.
- **Guide mistaken for Teacher** — Guides share *information*. Teachers
  share *persuasion*. Information isn't influence.
- **Blocker avoidance** — reps route around Blockers forever, which only
  works until the Blocker has veto at the end. Better: neutralize them
  through a peer who outranks them.

## Deal-level rollup

After scoring every contact, answer:

1. **How many Mobilizers do we have?** _____
   - If zero → **the deal is not real yet**. The next sprint is
     Mobilizer-creation, not progression.
2. **Are our Mobilizers connected to the Economic Buyer?** Y / N / Unknown
3. **Is our "champion" actually a Talker?** Y / N
4. **Who's the Blocker, and how do we route around them?** ____________
5. **Buying committee size (named stakeholders we've met)**: _____
   - Below 3 → we're under-mapped. Above 7 → consensus risk; double down
     on tailored economic-impact pack per role.

## Action rules

- **1+ Mobilizer with EB access** → deal can progress. Run MEDDPICC.
- **Mobilizer present but no EB access** → weekly focus on bridging.
- **No Mobilizer yet** → don't forecast. Re-run Warmer + Reframe with a new
  contact. Discovery-first won't find a Mobilizer where one doesn't exist.
- **Climber as primary contact in late stage** → escalate or lose.

## Cross-skill handoff

- Pass the scorecard into **MEDDPICC** for the Champion letter.
- Use **Never Split the Difference** (labels, mirrors) to get Skeptics to
  speak their real objections.
- Use **Winning by Design** handoffs when a Mobilizer converts — don't
  lose them in the transition to onboarding.
