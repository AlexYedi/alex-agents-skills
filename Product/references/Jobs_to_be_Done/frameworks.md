## Catalog

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
**Definition:** Customers hire products to make progress toward an objective.
**Three layers:** Functional / Emotional / Social.

### Job Performer
**Definition:** End user executing the job.
**Distinct from:** Buyer, Approver, Manager, Reviewer, Audience, Assistant.

### Causality over Correlation
**Principle:** Find why people act, not what demographics they are.

### Milkshake Marketing Reframe (Christensen)
**Story:** Milkshake hired for "make commute interesting; keep me full" — not for "shake."

### Jobs Interviews
**Method:** Solution-free; probe specifics; long silences.

### Switch Interview (Bob Moesta)
**Method:** Reverse-engineer switch decisions via the 6-phase timeline.

### Switch Timeline (6 Phases)
1. First Thought
2. Passively Looking
3. Actively Looking
4. Deciding
5. Consuming
6. Satisfaction

### Inverted Switch Interview
**Method:** Switch structure applied to cancellation.

### Customer Case Research (CCR)
**Origin:** Nitterhouse + Berstell.
**Method:** Long-form ethnographic; for blind spots; surfaces unarticulated needs.

### Four Forces of Progress
**Forces:** Push of situation + Pull of new solution vs Anxiety of new + Habit of present.
**Rule:** Change happens when (Push + Pull) > (Anxiety + Habit).

### Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI) — Tony Ulwick
**Phases:** Identify Job → Job Map → Define Outcomes → Quantify Market.

### Job Map (8 Phases) — Bettencourt + Ulwick
**Phases:** Define → Locate → Prepare → Confirm → Execute → Monitor → Modify → Conclude.

### Desired Outcome Statement
**Format:** Direction + Unit of measure + Object + Context.
**Example:** "Minimize the time it takes to identify approved suppliers given my company's policies."

### Opportunity Score
**Formula:** Importance + max(Importance - Satisfaction, 0).
**Use:** Rank desired outcomes for innovation prioritization.

### Job Stories
**Format:** "When [situation], I want [motivation], so I can [outcome]."
**Origin:** Intercom + Alan Klement.

### JTBD Canvas
**Inspired by:** Daniel Silverstein.
**Structure:** Job statement at top; functional + emotional needs below; performer + context + forces.

### Value Proposition Canvas
**Origin:** Osterwalder + Pigneur (Strategyzer).
**Structure:** Customer Profile (jobs/pains/gains) ↔ Value Map (pain relievers/gain creators/products).

### Hypothesis Progression Framework (HPF)
**Origin:** Lowdermilk + Rich (*The Customer-Driven Playbook*).
**Stages:** Customer → Problem → Concept → Feature.

### Goal-Driven Personas (Alan Cooper)
**Approach:** Personas defined by goals/jobs, not demographics.

### User Environment Design (UED)
**Method:** Model solution structure independent of UI for longevity.

### Consumption Journey Map
**Distinction:** Tracks interactions with your specific solution. (Job Map = main job; Consumption Journey = your product's journey.)

### JTBD Growth Strategy Matrix (Ulwick)
**Axes:** Better/Worse × Cheaper/More expensive.
**Quadrants:** WIN / GROW / DEFEND / DROP.

### Ansoff Matrix
**Axes:** Existing/New product × Existing/New market.
**JTBD lens:** Same job / different context.

### Surviving Disruption Canvas
**Origin:** Christensen + Wessel.
**Use:** Identify low-end disruption threats.

### Diffusion of Innovations (Everett Rogers)
**5 heuristics:** Relative advantage, Compatibility, Complexity, Trialability, Observability.

### Fogg Behavior Model
**Origin:** BJ Fogg.
**Equation:** Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt.

### Arenas Based on Jobs (Rita McGrath)
**Definition:** Markets defined by outcomes, not industries.

### Shared Value (Porter & Kramer)
**Linking:** Revenue creation + social benefit.

### Organizing Around Jobs
**Pattern:** Squads aligned to jobs; chapters for technical specialization (Spotify-inspired).

### Onboarding into the Job
**Principle:** Drive customers to first job completion (not feature tour).
**Metric:** Time to Value (TTV).

### Hierarchy of Abstraction
**Principle:** Same job can be stated at multiple levels; pick level matching the decision.
