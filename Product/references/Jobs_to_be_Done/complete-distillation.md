## 2. Executive Summary

Kalbach's contribution: **JTBD as a substrate** that runs through every part of the organization — research, strategy, product, marketing, support, org design. The book integrates the three main JTBD schools (Christensen / Ulwick / Moesta) into a practitioner playbook. Distinctive: the breadth — most JTBD books are research-focused; Kalbach extends to strategy, organization, onboarding, and support.

## 3. The Big Takeaways

1. **Customers hire products to make progress.** Job ≠ solution.
2. **Job Performer is the primary unit of analysis.**
3. **Functional first; emotional and social on top.**
4. **Causality over correlation.** Why people act, not what demographics they are.
5. **Solution-agnostic competition.** Tax software competes with pencils.
6. **Three research methods:** Jobs interviews, Switch interviews, CCR.
7. **Four Forces of Progress:** Push + Pull > Anxiety + Habit.
8. **Switch Timeline:** 6 phases of decision unfolding.
9. **Job Map:** 8 standard phases of a main job.
10. **Desired Outcome Statements:** direction + unit + object + context.
11. **Importance × Satisfaction → Opportunity score** for innovation prioritization.
12. **Job Stories operationalize; outcome statements prioritize.**
13. **JTBD Growth Strategy Matrix** (Ulwick): better/worse × cheaper/expensive.
14. **Diffusion of Innovations** + **Fogg Behavior Model** for adoption.
15. **Onboard into the job, not the product.** Time to Value matters.
16. **Inverted Switch interviews** for cancellation diagnosis.
17. **Squads aligned to jobs** for organizational structure.
18. **JTBD is a substrate, not a research method.**

## 7. Decision Rules Consolidated

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Customer research | Jobs interviews; solution-free |
| Understanding acquisition | Switch interviews on recent customers |
| Understanding churn | Inverted Switch interviews on cancellers |
| Suspected blind spot | CCR (longer ethnographic) |
| High-stakes innovation | Full ODI (4 phases; opportunity scoring) |
| Engineering brief | Job Stories format |
| Sprint planning | Job Stories per ticket |
| Persona work | Goal-based, not demographic |
| Adoption challenge | Four Forces analysis + Fogg + Diffusion of Innovations |
| Strategic positioning | JTBD Growth Strategy Matrix |
| Adjacency exploration | Ansoff with JTBD lens |
| Disruption threat | Surviving Disruption Canvas |
| Org design (30+ engineers) | Squads aligned to jobs |
| Onboarding | Drive to first job completion; minimize TTV |
| Support training | Listen for the job; resolution = job done |
| Big customer demands feature | Probe job; consider alternatives |
| Innovation roadmap | Drive from underserved outcomes (ODI) |
| Vague outcome statement | Refine to direction + unit + object + context |
| Vague lever | Make measurable; tie to job phase |

## 8. Anti-Patterns Consolidated

- Asking about the product instead of the job
- Demographic-first segmentation
- Skipping functional job
- Confusing buyer with performer (B2B)
- Switch interview as 30-minute survey
- Never doing cancellation interviews
- Generic personas built from demographics
- One job, forever (no refresh)
- Cutting corners on needs analysis (5 chats)
- Vague outcome statements
- Generic user stories without context
- One job map for everyone
- Personas decorated with stock photos
- Surveying importance without satisfaction
- Skipping the Job Map
- Static ODI output
- JTBD research without strategic application
- Design thinking only (no organizational change)
- Onboarding as feature tour
- Support that closes tickets, not jobs
- Functional silos slowing job delivery
- Innovation roadmap driven by loudest customer
