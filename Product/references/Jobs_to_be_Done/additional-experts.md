# Jobs to be Done Playbook — Additional Expert Notes

> Best practices from Jim Kalbach's *The Jobs to be Done Playbook* (Rosenfeld Media, 2020).

## About the Author

**Jim Kalbach** — Chief Evangelist at Mural; experienced UX strategist; long-time JTBD advocate. The Playbook synthesizes work from Christensen, Ulwick, Moesta, Klement, Bettencourt, and others. Voice: practitioner-friendly, integrative; doesn't pick a "true JTBD" sect.

## Foundational Mindset Shifts

1. **Jobs ≠ solutions.** Customers hire products to make progress.
2. **Job Performer is the unit of analysis.** Not buyer, not approver.
3. **Functional first; emotional and social on top.**
4. **Causality over correlation.** Why people act, not what demographics they are.
5. **Jobs are stable; solutions evolve.**
6. **Solution-agnostic competition.** Map by job, not category.
7. **Discover, don't invent.** Real interviews; not whiteboard speculation.
8. **JTBD is a substrate, not a research method.** Runs through strategy, marketing, product, support, org.

## Best Practices by Topic

### Research methods
- Jobs interviews: solution-free; probe specifics; long silences
- Switch interviews: 60-90 min; six-phase timeline; recent switchers
- Inverted Switch: same structure for cancellation diagnosis
- Customer Case Research (CCR): ethnographic; for blind spots
- 30-50 jobs interviews before claiming understanding (rule of thumb)
- Re-interview annually

### Innovation prioritization
- Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI) for high-stakes
- Desired Outcome Statements: direction + unit + object + context
- Importance × Satisfaction → Opportunity score
- Underserved outcomes are the gold; overserved are simplification candidates

### Modeling
- Job Map (8 phases): Define → Locate → Prepare → Confirm → Execute → Monitor → Modify → Conclude
- Each phase has its own outcomes
- Hierarchy of abstraction: pick the level matching the decision

### Engineering integration
- Job Stories ("When..., I want to..., so I can..."): situation + motivation + outcome
- JTBD Canvas one-pager for team alignment
- Value Proposition Canvas for positioning

### Persona work
- Goal-driven personas (Cooper); not demographic
- Lead with the job, not the photo

### Product strategy
- JTBD Growth Strategy Matrix (Ulwick): better/worse × cheaper/expensive
- Ansoff Matrix with JTBD lens
- Surviving Disruption Canvas for threat assessment
- Diffusion of Innovations heuristics for adoption

### Adoption / behavior
- Fogg Behavior Model: Motivation × Ability × Prompt
- Diffusion of Innovations: relative advantage + compatibility + low complexity + trialability + observability

### Organization
- Squads aligned to jobs; chapters for technical specialization (Spotify-inspired)
- Roles: Performer / Buyer / Approver / Manager / Reviewer / Audience / Assistant — distinguish

### Onboarding
- Onboard into the job, not the product
- Time to Value (TTV) is the metric
- First success in first session

### Support
- Listen for the job behind the question
- Resolution = job done, not ticket closed

### Retention
- Inverted Switch interviews on cancellers
- Account management focused on outcomes
- Surface job-completion metrics to customers

## Specific Advice

### "Solution-agnostic competition"
**Why:** Industry boundaries hide real competitive landscape.
**Apply:** Tax software competes with pencils, accountants, and skipping filing. Identify all alternatives that get the job done.

### "Functional first"
**Why:** Emotional/social can't be solved if functional fails.
**Apply:** Marketing emotional appeal? Confirm functional works first.

### "Discover, don't invent jobs"
**Why:** Internal speculation is rationalization. Real customers reveal real jobs.
**Apply:** 30-50 jobs interviews before declaring understanding.

### "Onboard into the job, not the product"
**Why:** Feature tours waste time-to-value.
**Apply:** Drive customer to first job completion in first session if possible.

### "Listen for the job in support"
**Why:** Customer asks how to do X; underlying need is Y.
**Apply:** Train support to confirm the job, not just answer the question.

### "Squads over silos"
**Why:** Customer outcomes cross functions. Silo coordination is taxing.
**Apply:** For 30+ engineer teams, squads aligned to jobs; chapters for technical discipline.

### "Inverted Switch for cancellation"
**Why:** Survey-based churn analysis is shallow. Switch reveals the real story.
**Apply:** 5-10 inverted switch interviews per quarter on representative cancellers.

### "Goal-driven personas"
**Why:** Demographics correlate weakly with jobs.
**Apply:** Personas led by the job; demographics for media targeting only.

### "Capture artifacts immediately"
**Why:** Memory and context erode.
**Apply:** Insight capture during interviews; synthesis within a week.

## Anti-Patterns Deeper Than Skill Files

- **Asking about the product** instead of the job
- **Demographic-first segmentation**
- **Skipping functional job** (emotional appeal first)
- **Confusing buyer with performer** (especially B2B)
- **Switch interview as 30-min survey**
- **Never doing cancellation interviews**
- **Generic personas built from demographics**
- **One job, forever** (not refreshing research)
- **Cutting corners on needs analysis** (5 chats; declare understanding)
- **Vague outcome statements** ("better X")
- **Generic user stories without context**
- **One job map for everyone**
- **Personas decorated with stock photos**
- **Surveying importance without satisfaction**
- **Skipping the Job Map**
- **Static ODI output**
- **JTBD research without strategic application**
- **Design thinking only** (no organizational practice change)
- **Onboarding as feature tour**
- **Support that closes tickets, not jobs**
- **Functional silos slowing job delivery**
- **Innovation roadmap driven by loudest customer**

## Process Wisdom

- Pre-interview prep: situation, recent moment, willing to be specific
- Probe the obvious — "tell me more about that"
- Watch for what they didn't say
- Capture quotes verbatim where possible
- Synthesize across 5-10 interviews before drawing conclusions
- Continuous research, not one-time projects
- Cross-functional alignment via shared JTBD vocabulary
- Roadmap presentations led by JTBD insight, not feature lists
